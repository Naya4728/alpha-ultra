SIX GEOPOLIITICAL ZONES IN NIGERIA

North Central

The North Central region of Nigeria, also known as the Middle Belt, consists of the following states:

  • Benue: Known as the "Food Basket of the Nation" due to its rich agricultural produce, especially yams, rice, and soybeans.
  • Kogi: Home to the confluence of the Niger and Benue rivers, and rich in natural resources like coal and limestone.
  • Kwara: Known for its diverse culture and the historic city of Ilorin, which is a major educational and commercial center.
  • Nasarawa: Rich in solid minerals and known for its agricultural activities, particularly in the production of sesame seeds and yams.
  • Niger: The largest state in Nigeria by land area, known for its hydroelectric power stations and the Kainji Dam.
  • Plateau: Known for its unique rock formations, temperate climate, and as a major tourist destination in Nigeria.
  • Federal Capital Territory (Abuja): The capital city of Nigeria, known for its well-planned infrastructure and as the political center of the country.

The North Central zone is characterized by a diverse mix of ethnic groups, including the Tiv, Idoma, Nupe, and Gwari, among others. This cultural diversity is reflected in the region's festivals, traditional dances, and cuisine. The people of this zone are known for their hospitality and communal lifestyle, which fosters a strong sense of unity and cooperation among the various ethnic groups.

Geographically, the North Central zone features a mix of savannah and forested areas, with a landscape that includes rolling hills, plateaus, and river valleys. The region's climate is generally moderate, with distinct wet and dry seasons. Agriculture is a major economic activity, with crops such as yams, rice, and maize being widely cultivated. Additionally, the zone is rich in mineral resources, including tin, limestone, and coal, which contribute to its economic development.

North East

The North East region of Nigeria is made up of the following states:

  • Adamawa: Known for its diverse culture and the historic city of Yola, which is a major commercial and educational center.
  • Bauchi: Home to the Yankari National Park, a popular tourist destination known for its wildlife and natural springs.
  • Borno: Known for its rich history and cultural heritage, including the ancient city of Maiduguri and the Kanuri people.
  • Gombe: Known for its agricultural activities, particularly in the production of cotton, groundnuts, and maize.
  • Taraba: Known for its diverse ethnic groups and scenic landscapes, including the Mambilla Plateau, which is known for its temperate climate and tea plantations.
  • Yobe: Known for its agricultural activities, particularly in the production of millet, sorghum, and livestock farming.

The North East zone is characterized by a diverse mix of ethnic groups, including the Kanuri, Fulani, and Hausa, among others. This cultural diversity is reflected in the region's festivals, traditional dances, and cuisine. The people of this zone are known for their resilience and strong sense of community, which has helped them to overcome various challenges.

Geographically, the North East zone features a mix of savannah and semi-arid areas, with a landscape that includes plains, hills, and river valleys. The region's climate is generally hot and dry, with a short rainy season. Agriculture is a major economic activity, with crops such as millet, sorghum, and maize being widely cultivated. Additionally, the zone is rich in natural resources, including limestone and gypsum, which contribute to its economic development.

South west

The South West region of Nigeria is made up of the following states:

  • Ekiti: Known for its agricultural activities, particularly in the production of cocoa, kolanuts, and palm oil.
  • Lagos: Known as the commercial capital of Nigeria, with a vibrant economy and diverse population.
  • Ogun: Known for its industrial activities, particularly in the production of cement, steel, and food products.
  • Ondo: Known for its rich cultural heritage and the historic city of Akure, which is a major educational and commercial center.
  • Osun: Known for its historic sites, including the Osun-Osogbo Sacred Grove, a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Oyo: Known for its rich history and cultural heritage, including the ancient city of Ibadan and the Yoruba people.

The South West zone is characterized by a diverse mix of ethnic groups, predominantly the Yoruba people. This cultural diversity is reflected in the region's festivals, traditional dances, and cuisine. The people of this zone are known for their entrepreneurial spirit and vibrant cultural expressions, which contribute to the dynamic atmosphere of the region.

Geographically, the South West zone features a mix of coastal areas, forests, and savannahs, with a landscape that includes hills, rivers, and lagoons. The region's climate is generally tropical, with a distinct wet and dry season. Agriculture is a major economic activity, with crops such as cocoa, kolanuts, and palm oil being widely cultivated. Additionally, the zone is a hub for industrial and commercial activities, particularly in Lagos, which is the economic powerhouse of Nigeria.

South East

  • Imo: Known for its rich cultural heritage and the historic city of Owerri, which is a major tourist destination.

The South East zone is characterized by a predominantly Igbo ethnic group, known for their rich cultural heritage and entrepreneurial spirit. This cultural diversity is reflected in the region's festivals, traditional dances, and cuisine. The people of this zone are known for their industrious nature and strong sense of community, which fosters a vibrant and dynamic atmosphere.

Geographically, the South East zone features a mix of forested areas and river valleys, with a landscape that includes hills and plains. The region's climate is generally tropical, with a distinct wet and dry season. Agriculture is a major economic activity, with crops such as palm oil, yams, and cassava being widely cultivated. Additionally, the zone is known for its industrial activities, particularly in the production of textiles, shoes, and pharmaceuticals, which contribute to its economic development.

South South

The South South region of Nigeria is made up of the following states:

  • Akwa Ibom: Known for its rich oil reserves and the historic city of Uyo, which is a major commercial and educational center.
  • Bayelsa: Known for its oil-rich Niger Delta region and the historic city of Yenagoa, which is a major economic and political center.
  • Cross River: Known for its diverse culture and the historic city of Calabar, which is a major tourist destination.
  • Delta: Known for its oil-rich Niger Delta region and the historic city of Warri, which is a major industrial and commercial center.
  • Edo: Known for its rich cultural heritage and the historic city of Benin, which is a major educational and commercial center.
  • Rivers: Known for its oil-rich Niger Delta region and the historic city of Port Harcourt, which is a major industrial and commercial center.

The South South zone is characterized by a diverse mix of ethnic groups, including the Ijaw, Ibibio, and Efik, among others. This cultural diversity is reflected in the region's festivals, traditional dances, and cuisine. The people of this zone are known for their hospitality and vibrant cultural expressions, which contribute to the dynamic atmosphere of the region.

Geographically, the South South zone features a mix of coastal areas, mangrove swamps, and rainforests, with a landscape that includes rivers, creeks, and estuaries. The region's climate is generally tropical, with a distinct wet and dry season. Agriculture and fishing are major economic activities, with crops such as oil palm, rubber, and cassava being widely cultivated. Additionally, the zone is rich in oil and gas resources, which contribute significantly to Nigeria's economy.
